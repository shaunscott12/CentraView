Where to Start

Since every bit of business information stored in CentraView is capable of interacting with every other piece of business information, where do you start to enter information?

Adding Contacts

A good starting point is to enter detailed information for all your business contacts.  Contact information gives you a ready database of email addresses, phone numbers, mobile contacts, physical addresses, and any other method of maintaining vital business communications.

First, add entities, which are company or organizations you do business with, possibly as buyers, suppliers or vendors.  Next, add individuals, who may be employees of the companies you do business with, individual vendors, employees of your own company or any person who you contact about business.  You can then create logical groupings for your entities and individuals.  

Any contact information you enter will be accessible when you are composing an email, scheduling activities, inviting participants to marketing events, or any other business activity.

Scheduling Activities

When you have an important activity coming up, you'll want to keep track of it using CentraView's incorporated calendar system.  Whether scheduling a phone call, an appointment, or a meeting, CentraView automatically adds the activity to your business calendar.  CentraView posts reminders to your home page and will even forward invitations via email to all of your participants.

To add activities to your business calendar, select the Activities tab and choose the appropriate activity type.

Appointments, calls and meetings all involve other participants and usually have specific start times.  For these activities, CentraView will post reminders, send invitations via email, and track progress and resolutions.

Next actions, to dos and tasks are usually less time dependent, and could be part of a larger goal.  CentraView allows you to tie these actions into other, larger activities.

Literature requests are one-time activities, usually involving the mailing or transferring of information from the business for clarifying purposes.

Forecast sales relate directly to sales opportunities and are extremely useful for tracking and performance purposes.
